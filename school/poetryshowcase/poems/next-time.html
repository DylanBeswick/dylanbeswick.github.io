My First Time Ziplining

A Poem Written by Dylan Beswick

I rip around
the snow, snow
flying back behind
me. I cut new trails in
an instant and use them
until the snow is all worn off.
I cut through the snow like
a knife. I could never go
as fast on foot. I felt
the wind rushing
past, and the
speeding feeling.
I could do this forever.
But all good things must
come to an end, because my
parents say it’s time to go, after
only five minutes of fun. They say
it’s five o’clock, and I haven’t
had lunch yet, so we had to
leave. I will bring lunch
the next time we
go snowmobiling.

Dylan Beswick 2016
