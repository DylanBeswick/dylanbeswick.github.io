My First Time Ziplining

A Poem Written by Dylan Beswick

I shudder as I climb the ladder.
I don’t know if I’ll survive.
Will I be lucky to come out alive?
After weeks of climbing, I finally reach the top.
And I look down to the ground, just a nine thousand meter drop.
I let the attendant put a harness on me, all nice and tight.
I guess I’m just too scared to put up a real fight.
When I finally wake from my daze of terror, it is too late.
Is this really going to be my fate?
Somehow I’m trembling, hanging from the line.
It didn’t help when they said I’d be fine.
My muscles are like concrete, they’re so tense.
Why did I do this? I’m so dense.
My harness hugs me, while I think about my doom.
Suddenly the attendant pulled a string…
SHOOM
I open my eyes at 5 trillion meters from the ground.
When I fall, how many decades will it take me to be found?
I feel the air rushing past, and the sun’s heat.
Wait. I admit it. This is actually kind of sweet.
I zip along the rest of the way feeling as bold as ten grown men.
Thinking the whole time “I’m definitely doing this again"
